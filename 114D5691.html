Work

The summer started off interestingly when the people drilling holes in the floor to bring equipment to the roof for geothermal work accidentally drilled a hole through the wires that bring electricity to my office, so I was a nomad for a couple of days. This was not aptly timed as I was in the middle of doing one-on-one study sessions, but we made it work. Then the whole lab got displaced because they are waxing the floors of the labs on the dry side of Ford, so we have set up camp in 342 which makes me feel like I'm in class.

We've finished all of the non-stretch goals for the GitLab project, so we are working on deploying our version to Smith's servers and, expectedly, running into a bunch of errors. I've also been working on an odd conundrum in which the tool works on 2/3 of the lab computers but mysteriously none of the functionality we have implemented shows up on the third computer, despite having the same code and same versions of everything we have thought to check. I'm feeling very unready to leave the lab; there's still too many projects I want to be a part of and the fact that half the lab graduated made me very sad because I love the social dynamic in the lab.

We are finished up a workshop paper that combines studies ran in the fall and spring with the high schooler's project. The other paper I submitted to RE got rejected today so this is now my only hope.

The end of the school year also created many fun diversions during the work day. It was thesis season and there's little that I love more than listening to people who I may or may not know present their theses. I also attended the ice cream freeze off, went out to lunch with the seniors in the lab, and attended Jessica's senior recital and Into the Woods. Unfortunately the summer will likely be more monotonous.

Weekends

I had a rare planless weekend and took a tour of the Emily Dickinson museum, which I had meant to do for a while ever since watching the show Dickinson. We got a tour of Emily's parents house, which has been restored to look as similar as possible to how it did when they lived there, and also of Emily's brother's house, which is being preserved as much as possible with what was originally in the house. It was really interesting and I learned a lot about the messiness and controversy surrounding the publication of her poems.

Katie and Chelsea came to Northampton for Sarah's thesis and after work we all went to the Tunnel Bar. The next morning I said goodbye to Aerin at Muffin House, watched Arsenal crash out of the Champion's League in the Milford Town Library, and then met Katie, Chelsea, and Katie's roommates in JP for the Wake Up the Earth festival. Chelsea and I realized that we had learned about the I-95 protests that the festival celebrates in our landscape studies class, which was a fun crossover. That night, we watched the Kentucky Derby and played a bunch of Fill or Bust. I briefly caught up with Riley the next morning and then returned to Muffin House for breakfast with Seychelle, Maggie, and Burgs, followed by a dominant pickleball performance. Maggie, Seychelle, and I went to a Boston Legacy game in the afternoon. They came back with goals in the 89th and 90+4th to earn their first-ever win as a club which was electric.

Last weekend, my dad and I went for a brief one-night backpacking trip in the Glastenbury Wilderness in Vermont. It rained for the entire first day which was not pleasant and offered no views, but there was no one else at the campsite which is just the way I like it. We cowboy camped which I had never done but was nice accept for a small mammal (possum? raccoon? squirrel?) who visited in the middle of the night. The next morning, we summitted Glastenbury Mountain, which has a fire tower from which you are supposedly able to see the most wilderness area from anywhere in Vermont, but there were many signs saying that it was no longer structurally sound, so we did not get to see the view. The hike out was nice though, and then my dad and I drove back and went to Florence Pizza.

This past weekend was Commencement at Smith. Several members of my class were on campus during senior week which was fun. Fruit Street had a lovely gathering with their families (plus me and Mo) on Friday night and then we went to Packard's with the siblings. I witnessed my first outdoor Ivy Day and then took a nap and then went to Spreen dinner. Then we went to Illuminations and the alum dance party on Chapin Lawn. They graduated on Sunday and then we had to say the most terrible goodbyes.
