Work

Shortly after the last newsletter, we submitted our paper to RE@Next!. I celebrated by going to see Project Hail Mary which I might make a tradition because movies that I want to see in theaters seem to come out at roughly the same pace that I submit papers.

Then, we immediately started working on revisions for the journal paper we had submitted in the fall. This invovled debating a lot of statistics which I do enjoy but also tends to bleed out of my work life and into my weekend life. We submitted those revisions last week.

We also are starting to lock in on the workshop paper with the high school student (long story).

And GitRE is allegedly going to be done and deployed by the end of the semester, so I'm locking in on that too since the undergrads are all pretty busy with classwork right now.

I had to give a talk for the Sigma Xi lunch series. I was very nervous about giving a talk to so many professors that I know (logically, this should be less scary than giving a talk to experts in the field at a conference, but knowing the people made it feel like I was being more personally judged). It was okay though and I got a very wide range of questions and many more than I expected.

This week, we had a grad school reveal party during lab meeting, in which Jessica and I baked cakes with the color of our chosen grad school. She's going to the Peabody Conservatory.

Weekends

Prior to flying out to Toronto (see the next section), I went to watch my cousin's elementary school performance of Finding Nemo, the bilingual musical. She was an octopus and a seagull, and per her usual stage persona she did not crack a smile through the entire show, though the volume of her only line delivery was excellent. Then I went back to my cousins' house and we played Wavelength and Scattergories and then had dinner with my grandparents.

My parents visited because I needed to use the tax software on my dad's computer after Smith gave me two W-2s, one for Connecticut and one for Massachusetts, and the free online tax software were unable to figure out that both states were withholding from the same wages and kept trying to double the income they thought I made. I made them lunch as a bribe for driving up here, and after I finished the taxes we went for a walk at Fitzgerald Lake Conservation Area. Then Aerin and Anna were visiting so we went to Vera. Then Aerin and I went to Stella's band's concert. The music was good but I felt pretty weird being in Seelye. Sky and I had a mini Sunday dinner because everyone else was gone.

Katie and Chelsea came to visit for the senior thesis dance show. We went to Packard's with Sarah after. On Saturday, we did the obligatory Tunic run and then spent time in the woods. We sat atop Pony Mountain and then Katie went into the very cold water at Chapel Falls. Katie had to leave that night, but the next day Chelsea and I built the Lego set that we won back in December, played a bunch of cards, and then browsed their uncle's store in Chicopee.

Marathon

This morning, I ran a marathon in Newport. The first 15 miles felt great and the next 11 miles sucked, but my overall time (4:27:35) was faster than I was expecting. And I was the second (out of three total) non-binary finishers! The weather was great and the route went through nice neighborhoods along the water so I can't complain. My wonderful and supportive friends Aerin, Anna, Annie, Madelyn, Maggie, and Soph came and watched and then we hung out in Newport afterwards, mostly around a fire pit at a random resort and then we did the Cliff Walk.

Grad Visit: University of Toronto

I was really nervous for the week in which I visited the University of Toronto and Michigan State University. I had been referring to this week as 'Hell Week' and even though I ended up enjoying many parts of it, I still slightly stand by that classification due to the large quantity of travel and small quantity of sleep.

I landed in Toronto before 9am, which was weird because I had been up for seven hours but the city was still pretty quiet. I walked along the water for a little bit and then went to the aquarium because I figured that looking at fish might relieve some of my stress about all the socializing. Then I got went to the public library to do some work and pass the time before I could check into the hotel, where I promptly took a nap. On the first night, we had a reception and dinner in the hotel that I enjoyed more than I thought I was going to. I greatly enjoyed the mix of nerdy computer science jokes and non-science interests that I shared with the current and admitted students.

The second day of the grad visit was packed. We had a series of short presentations from different offices in the morning, most of which were a little bit boring, and then we had lunch in an old castley building. This was the first place I met my potential supervisor in person, as well as a fourth year PhD student in the lab, so I got to ask some more specific questions about the lab. In the afternoon, I had a one-on-one with my potential supervisor. Then I went to the DCS Women event which was a bit controversial in my head, but it sounds like most women are the only woman in their group except if the supervisor is a woman, so I was hoping it could be more of a gender minority situation, but I don't I will be returning to future events. Then someone from my lab and I walked around a bit and I got to get another perspective on the lab. For dinner, the PLSE (programming languages and software engineering) group went to an establishment called Snakes and Lattes, which apparently is a chain within Toronto and is a genius combination of board games, cafe, and bar. Then, a few of us went out to a bar and watched the Maple Leafs defeat the Bruins, to everyone's happiness except mine.

The final day was more informal; we had a breakfast bingo and then a campus tour from a couple of current grad students. My group's meeting happened to be on Wednesdays, so I attended that meeting and got to see them working which was nice because most of the weekend was clearly the department putting on a bit of a show beyond normal operations. Then I went out for coffee with a couple of people from the lab who had some free time. I still had some time to kill before my flight so I walked to a park (I was in desperate need to see a tree by this point) and then went to a vegetarian place in Chinatown for dinner. My flight ended up being delayed by two hours, causing me to have to wait until the 1:15am Logan Express, which was not great given the limited amount of time I had in New England.

Grad Visit: Michigan State University

The next morning, I got up and hustled out of my grandparents' house near Boston and drove to Hartford to fly to Detroit and then Lansing. The second flight was delayed which caused me to spend an unnecessarily long amount of time less than a two hour drive from my intended desination. My potential supervisor picked me up from the airport and we got dinner at an Indian restaurant. We once again yapped for two hours about both research and life; I am so good at talking to her.

The next day was crazy. I had thought that this visit was going to be a bit more informal since it wasn't a department-wide visit day and originated with me asking if I could just show up, but upon arrival I was handed a schedule of one-on-one meetings that booked me to the minute. My potential supervisor gave me a driving tour of the campus. The campus is many acres but most of it isn't built up. I really enjoy all of the greenery and trees between buildings, and the contained campus like Smith. Then I had one-on-ones with the graduate director of the CSE department and the associate dean of the grad school. They were both nice but I feel like I got more valuable information from the students. I had meetings with the two students who work in the lab. I hadn't previously realized that there were only two students. It also seems like undergrads don't really do research there, which is weird to me but not necessarily a pro or a con. I also had a meeting with another student who works at the center that joins computer science and biology. I went out to lunch at a Thai place with three women from various labs at various stages of their programs and then they took me into the main library. It was interesting to hear the differences between their labs. Then I went out to dinner at an Ethiopian restauarant with the current members of the lab and the supervisor, which was nice because I got to see them interact more organically.

Grad School Decision

I came out of the crazy week feeling good about either school that I visited. I made an extensive comparison list on the whiteboard in my office and thought I had it figured out, but when it came to actually submit my acceptance and declines, I had a slight crisis about it on April 14 but I submitted them and sent very scary emails anyway. I celebrated my decisiveness (though coerced) by swimming at Musante Beach for the first time of the season and attempting to make banana bread (which was foiled by a multi-hour power outage). Now that the decision has been made, I feel a little better now that it's out of my control.

So, this is my formal announcement to all of you that I will be attending the University of Toronto in the fall. Time to learn French and watch Nirvanna: The Band: The Show: The Movie. Yay!
